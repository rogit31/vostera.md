This is a meta document that is a resource to build more interesting combat encounters. 

# Core Elements

The core elements of an interesting combat can generally be boiled down to a few different parts.

## The Objective

Generally speaking, the most traditional combat objective is to simply kill what is in front of them. However, having the same objective over and over again can get boring. Think of a video game were all the missions are fetch quests for example. Here are some different ones.

### Kill Everything

Even in the most standard form of combat, there is still some variety. Notably, how can characters or NPCs avoid dying? As that is generally speaking, not something any creature wants to do. It is very rare in fact that combattants will try to not negotiate for their lives in someway. They can do so by:

- Fleeing
- Taking someone or something hostage
- Surrendering
- Bribing 
- Lying
- Distracting
- Calling for reinforcements

Before a combat, you can establish which one of these each member of the baddies will attempt to do before being defeated when they realize they're on the losing end of a battle.

### Kill the Target

A slight variation where the objective in the combat is to kill or neutralize a specific individual(s) to end the combat. Make it clear eliminating the target will effectively end the combat in some way or another. This often ends up being a boss fight or assassination. 

### Protect the Target

The reverse to the previous objective, protect a specific target at all costs. A prince of a far-away kingdom needs your help, or a NPC your players are particularly fond of is wanted by some bandits. This forces players to focus on a different objective than just killing what's in front of them as efficiently as possible. 

### Save the Hostages

This time the target is already in the hands of the enemy and you must rescue them, and the enemies might negotiate their hostages against you. This particularly rewards creative decision making in deception, insight, and stealth as well as combat so works particularly well if you like combat encounters that are not just crunchy.

### Stop the Flood

A flood of enemies that will not stop coming through a variety of obstacles that can be manipulated in some way, like doors or bridges. This is a zombie apocalypse that must be stopped. 

### Escape

Pretty self explanatory, sometimes the goal of the combat is to avoid it entirely and to leave by making as much distance as possible. Pair with some interesting terrain and obstacles that are interactable.

### Stop the Ritual

Some evil ritual is happening and it must be stopped by any means, because otherwise the PC's cat dies or something. Have clear conditions for which the ritual will stop and a deadline it must be stopped by. 

### Get the MacGuffin

Get the thing! The Ring! The Book! Whatever it is, there's a thing somewhere and both the heroes and the baddies want it and they have to fight to get it first and leave with it.

### Pull the Lever

Basically a MacGuffin that does something, and that doesn't move. It can be a trap or a nexus of power or a spawning beacon for example. 

## Optimizers

Things that can help the combat go better. This is generally a great way of adding some complexity, because you have to make a decision in between spending actions now directly doing damage or spending those actions in favor of having a better position or state later. 

### Position

Positioning can be anything that enables players to be in a spot where they can hit the baddies more and get hit less themselves. This generally resolves to: 

- High Ground
- Visibility
- Cover

### The Stash

A stash of things that contains a bunch of items or things of interest. This usually can be one, or some/all of:

- Potions
- Magical weapons
- Scrolls
- Bombs
- Protective gear or armor


### The Chevy Chase

Two separate areas with a divider in between them, and some entrances that can be closed or open in between them. Add an entrance that cannot be closed to differentiate it in between the stop the flood objective above. Add a height differential in between them to make it even more interesting. 


### Levers and Traps

The optimizer here is basically just a person using a bunch of traps and bullshit to fuck with the players, and make it clear that the players can also take control of it to fuck with their enemies as well. Rocks falling from the ceiling, trap doors with pits under them, etc etc. 

### The big Gun

Yeah it's exactly what it sounds like. A ballista, cannons, siege equipment, whatever. Make sure it is static and highly unweildy so that players can't just take it with them.

### Boon

A vat of poison that players can dip their weapons in, or some blessed water that will defeat the ghosts faster. A temporary buff to the players or their gear that works for like a round, or just for the combat. Or like a temporary HP zone or something.

## Hazards

The classic red barrel in a video-game.

### No-go zone

You go there, you die. Show these clearly and be sure to use them sparingly. A skyship, a crack or canyon in the battlefield, a well, the roaring magma of a volcano. This immediately raises the lethality of a combat so make sure to communicate that with your players accordingly.

### Vat of Acid

Instead of a no-go zone, where you immediately die, this is extraordinarily bad, but not life-threateningly so, you might make it out before you die. Some examples includes:

- a vat of acid
- lava 
- pit of creatures
- Magical machinery somewhere
- stampede or rushing river

### On a roof

Self-explanatory.

### Thin Ice

Once again quite self-explanatory, use the DMG's rules combined with character and creature sizes to use that. Doesn't have to be ice, can be quicksand or glass as well.

## Chaos

Something to just, add some spice. A little something something going on that can make the combat more unpredictable but not necessarily a part of the combat design itself. Some classics include:

### Fire

A bunch of flammable materials, some exploding kegs whatever, have shit on fire.

### Darkness

Magical or natural darkness can be tricky to play around. Pairs will with the pull the lever macguffin, for example if you have a magical object emanating some magical darkness you can have the players interact with it to turn it on or off. 

### Random Stash

The players don't know what the stash does. 


### Bring it Down

Bring the roof or the structure of the combat down to a crumble. Maybe the cave is being crushed underneath the weight of a lake above it, or the skyship is going down. Either way, the current structure of the combat is not long for this world. 

### Unlabeled Levers

The levers from earlier are unlabeled and you're not sure what they do.

### Let them out

A gigantic creature is kept in a cage nearby, or a powerful wizard is magically handcuffed. Except this time, you're not sure whether or not they're your allies or not. Sometimes they explicitly are a third unbiased party that will kill whatever.
