## Meta

Noir-flavored two session minimum story arc set in an alternate reality of earth where the feywild and earth are in a cold war. The plot is mostly focused on a murder mystery, with both social and combat encounters, but very little exploration. Using the 2024/5.5 edition dungeons and dragons rules.

## Vibe

The vibe of a noir is inherently dark, gritty and serious. Substance abuse is rampant, everyone is mildly depressed, people are pessimistic at heart. However, we are borrowing the tropes and language of a noir movie without inherently adopting all of it. This won't be game of thrones, but I also don't expect it to be rick and morty. Somewhere in the middle of the silly-meter. I sometimes like to use gory descriptions, please let me know if you're not ok with it. 

# Premise

In 1948, Aleksandar Kutzenov, a half elven Russian man is the first person to walk to the north pole. There he finds a glass cube. Against the advice of his crew, he touches it. It shatters immediately, expanding and contracting until it reshapes as a sphere about 10 meters wide, floating a meter above the ground. On the other side, a world similar in geography to earth, and yet completely different. The feywild. 

Things start changing quickly on earth, people of all kinds of species, backgrounds and ages start exhibiting magical powers. The world has yet to adapt to the rise of magic, and even more so the presence of a foreign world right on top of ours. Scientists discover that the sphere, now called the junction, is expanding. Scientists forecast about 3 centuries until the two worlds fully merge together. Both earth and the feywild have a range of opinions about what is now being called the merge. Some look forward to a cooperation in between worlds, a world of possibilities to be open. Others look at the it with fear, what is preventing the feywild from killing us all? Is technology advanced enough to overcome the powerful magic of the feywild should conflict break? Is co-operation in between worlds even possible? 

Over the past two decades, slowly, the feywild and earth have been inching towards co-operation in measured, minuscule increments. Trade agreements are being drafted, and occasional envoys and diplomats from either world cross the junction to politicize, though the regular joe can only dream of such an opportunity. 

 It's a difficult process, and every step forward is met with one step back. Relationships sour as the feywild discover the concept of the atomic bomb, and exactly how many the USSR and the US have at their disposal. Similarly, tensions heighten on earth as news is leaked from the CIA that the fey have access to physic-defying magic such as time-stop, and are highly guarding the secrets to such powerful spells. In an effort to relieve the tensions in between worlds, UN diplomats and the high fey council decide to host a special event: the Interjunctional Exhibition. Representatives from both earth and the feywild will meet to compete in a variety of events, boxing, running, chess, archery, and more. ## Your characters

- Must have a reason to be there. Are you a journalist reporting on the event? Are you an athlete yourself? You can be either from the feywild or earth, both are totally a -ok. But either way, you must have some kind of reason to be invited to this party, or to be there for another reason (like a job for example, as a server, cook... etc). 
- Cannot be anti-social or a 'lone wolf' type character. Your character must be willing to cooperate to some degree, even if its against their personality or whateva. 
- Must have a reason to care about someone's murder. Are they a generally empathetic person? Are they a fan of Agatha Christie? Do they think the truth is important inherently? Do they have a background as law enforcement?
- Are somewhere in the middle of the silly-meter. Do not make complete jokes as characters (a sentient tobacco pipe) and give them actual names. However, feel free to make characters that are funny or interesting (communist dwarf rogue who believes pickpocketing the rich is the correct interpretation of marxism). Do NOT be afraid to use voices, referential humor, or blatantly steal your idea for your character from a piece of media you like (just don't say which!). 
- Must be ready by the session itself. If this is your first time making a dnd character, feel free to ask for help. I highly recommend using dndbeyond if it's your first time or you are not up to date on the rules. But you must be ready to play by the time the session starts. I will invite you all to a dndbeyond campaign so you guys can get access to all the extra content I have at my disposal. 

## Banned spell-list

The following spells are banned (and in-world do not exist) because simply put, they completely invalidate a murder mystery and narrative stakes. 
- Any spell that is "truth-telling" like zone-of truth
- Any spell that has a foretelling element to it, like divination, legend lore, foresight etc...
- Any reanimation spell (resurrection, revivify)
- Silvery barbs (hell no)
